Hi! I'm Aadit. I teach evidence-based tools and skills one can use to heal, grow, and thrive.

My practice is shaped by scientifically rigorous theories of mind and the deep passion I have for asking and answering hard questions about lives well-lived. I'm very glad you're here!

Focus Areas:

Self

The task of understanding oneself begins with the realization that our perceptions of what we believe to be true about our world, including the stories that we tell about ourselves, our flaws, insecurities, and fears, are reflections not necessarily of the world in and of itself, but of our own minds, and our emotional histories. In our work together, we will be conducting an audit of the contents of our minds and our belief structures, in order to begin the process of judicious evaluation of what may be true, and what might be maladaptive—where we may be getting in our own way. Others

In his Interpersonal Theory, American psychiatrist Harry Stack Sullivan sketched out his belief that human personalities are constructed within social contexts. In other words, without other people around to shape us, we would not have personalities. I hold that working on your relationship to others: how you might esteem their opinions, or not; the ways you might feel safe to be vulnerable around your loved ones, or not; how you may value your interpersonal connections, or not; can have revolutionary effects on every area of life. About Aadit:

I began my training as a psychologist in Integrated Attachment Theory (IAT) with Dr. Thais Gibson in 2020, and completed my certification as an IAT coach in early 2024. Prior to opening my counseling and coaching private practice, I worked as an MBB- strategy consultant in New York City, and held a research appointment with behavioral economists Dr. Karla Hoff and Nobel-prizewinner Dr. Joseph Stiglitz at Columbia University.

I was educated at the United World College of South-East Asia (2012-2014) and Yale-NUS College (2015-2017) in Singapore; Deep Springs College in California (2017-2019); and Columbia University in New York City (2019-2024), where I was the TOMS Scholar. My academic history has been expansive and interdisciplinary—spanning the liberal arts and sciences, and focussing on the humanities, intellectual history, philosophy, behavioral economics, and psychology.
