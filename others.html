Relationship with Others

The European Psychoanalyst Sigmund Freud believed that of all forms of suffering, that which we feel in our interpersonal relationships is the most devastating. Meaningful connections with others, and deep, resilient love, are emperically demonstrated cornerstones of mental health. But healthy love is not just a feelingâ€”it is also a teachable skill.

I take a results-based approach to helping clients thrive in their relationships. The product of our sessions is a gestalt: Clarity and confidence within yourself, along with the relational tools you need to go out and build the relationships you desire.

Key processes in this focus area include:

  • Building robust self-esteem, and overcoming the fear of rejection;
  • Undertanding one's needs in relationships;
  • Learning how to communicate needs and boundaries in relationships, in healthy ways;
  • Moving to secure attachment in intimate relationships;
  • Developing productive and compassionate conflict resolution skills;
  • Ending codependency and enmeshment, and building healthy mutual interdependence;
  • Healing from breakups, releasing resentment, and learning to truly forgive;
  • Transforming grief and loss;
  • Overcoming loneliness;
  • Mastering deep, vulnerable, and safe connections.
